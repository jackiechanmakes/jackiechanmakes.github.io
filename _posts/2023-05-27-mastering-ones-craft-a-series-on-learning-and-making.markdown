I’m completely enthralled by books written by craftsmen detailing their thought processes in how they master their crafts, which have included software engineering, wood working, knitting, robot building, and everything in between with the defining point being the act of putting something in the world that didn’t exist before. I’ve read *Every Tool’s a Hammer: Life Is What You Make It* by Adam Savage and *A Craftsman’s Legacy: Why Working with Our Hands Gives Us Meaning* by Eric Gorges and Jon Sternfield. I’m finishing up *Apprenticeship Patterns: Guidance for the Aspiring Software Craftsman* by Dave Hoover and Adewale Oshineye and am already looking forward to reading *Master Your Craft: Strategies for Designing, Making, and Selling Artisan Work* by Tien Chiu. I’m not sure why books on crafts have such long titles, but I love them because I learn so much from them.

As I read more and understand further how craftsmen work towards mastering their crafts, I’ve gained ample perspective on how I can make faster and more meaningful gains in my chosen craft of software engineering so much so that I’ve decided to create a series of posts on the subject matter. Here’s to furthering learning how to learn and making higher-quality things!
