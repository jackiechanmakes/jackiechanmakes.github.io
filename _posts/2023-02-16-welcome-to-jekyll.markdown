Yes.

Such a simple answer, yet it took me close to 10 years to figure this out because, until recently, I didn't understand the difference between coding and programming.

I knew I liked software engineering; otherwise, I wouldn't have kept relentlessly pursuing it through the years of self studying, official college classes, online courses, and computer science certificates. But let's be real - I knew I was drawn to software engineering because I kept wanting to hang out with the software engineers at my company and thought they were the coolest when I was on the data analytics team.


For some context, I graduated with a B.S. in Chemical Engineering from Caltech, where I extensively took part in biomaterials tissue engineering research. To this day, I still have so much love for chemical engineering and scientific research. After graduating college, I delved into the Data Analytics tech world. Data analytics was a huge part of my engineering research background, so I was fascinated with the idea of how my skills would be translated to the corporate world building products that would make it to market.
